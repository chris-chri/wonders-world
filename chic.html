Mexico, Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza
Chichen Itza is a renowned archaeological site located on the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico. It was a major city built by the Maya civilization and served as a significant political, economic, and religious center from approximately 600 AD to 1200 AD. The site is famous for its impressive architecture, including the iconic pyramid of Kukulcán (El Castillo), the Temple of the Warriors, and the Great Ball Court.
Palacio de Bellas Artes
The Palacio de Bellas Artes, or Palace of Fine Arts, is a prominent cultural center in Mexico City. Its construction began in 1904 under the direction of Italian architect Adamo Boari, who designed the building in an Art Nouveau and Neoclassical style.
Templo de Santo Domingo Oaxaca
The Templo de Santo Domingo de Guzmán in Oaxaca, Mexico, is a stunning example of Baroque architecture. Construction began in 1572 and was completed in 1608. The church was built by the Dominican Order and features an intricate facade and lavishly decorated interior. Over the centuries, it has served various purposes, including as a military warehouse and a stable. In 1979, it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage site along with the Historic Center of Oaxaca.
Columna De La Independencia
The Columna de la Independencia, also known as the Angel of Independence, is a victory column located on Paseo de la Reforma in Mexico City. It was built in 1910 during the presidency of Porfirio Díaz to commemorate the centennial of the beginning of Mexico's War of Independence.
