Italy, Vatican City

Colosseum

The Colosseum was built as a gift to the Roman people and served as a grand arena for gladiatorial combats, wild animal fights, and other public spectacles. Measuring approximately 620 by 513 feet, it was the largest amphitheater of its time and could hold up to 50,000 spectators

Vatican City
Vatican City, the smallest independent state in the world, is deeply intertwined with the Roman Catholic Church. Its origins date back to the 4th century AD when Emperor Constantine I began constructing a basilica over St. Peter's tomb.
Leaning Tower Of Pisa
The Leaning Tower of Pisa, also known as Torre Pendente di Pisa, is a freestanding bell tower of the cathedral in Pisa. The tower began to lean during construction due to the soft ground and inadequate foundation, which couldn't support its weight. Despite efforts to correct the tilt, the lean continued to worsen over the centuries.
Milian Cathedral
The Milan Cathedral is not only a religious site but also a cultural and historical landmark, attracting millions of visitors each year. Its central location in Milan's Piazza del Duomo makes it a focal point of the city and a symbol of Milanese pride and heritage.
