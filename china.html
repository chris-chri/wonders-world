Great Wall Of China
-The Great Wall of China is an ancient series of walls and fortifications, totaling more than 13,000 miles in length, located in northern China. Its construction began in the 7th century BC, with various states building walls to protect their territories. The first emperor of China, Qin Shi Huang, unified these walls around 221 BC to defend against invasions from northern nomadic tribes. The Great Hall Of The People
The Great Hall of the People is a significant state building located on the western side of Tiananmen Square in Beijing, China. It was constructed between 1958 and 1959 as part of the "Ten Great Buildings" project to commemorate the 10th anniversary of the founding of the People's Republic of China. The hall was designed by a team of prominent Chinese architects, including Zhang Bo, Chen Zhanxiang, and Zhang Jinqiao, and was completed in just 10 months
Shanghai Skyline
The Shanghai Skyline is one of the most iconic and recognizable cityscapes in the world. It features a stunning array of skyscrapers and architectural marvels, primarily located in the Lujiazui area of the Pudong district. The skyline includes notable landmarks such as the Oriental Pearl Tower, Jin Mao Tower, Shanghai World Financial Center, and the Shanghai Tower, which is the tallest building in China and the second tallest in the world
Zhangjiajie National Park
Zhangjiajie National Forest Park also features the world's tallest outdoor lift, the Bailong Elevator, which transports visitors to the top of the sandstone pillars. The park's diverse landscape includes deep ravines, lush forests, and numerous streams, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike
