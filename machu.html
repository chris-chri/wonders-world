Peru,Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu
-Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. Often referred to as the "Lost City of the Incas," it is one of the most iconic symbols of the Inca Empire. The site was built around 1450 during the height of the Inca Empire but was abandoned just over a century later, likely due to the Spanish conquest.
Arequipa
- Arequipa, known as the "White City" due to its stunning white volcanic stone architecture, is the second most populous city in Peru. Founded on August 15, 1540, by Garcí Manuel de Carbajal, it is situated at the foot of three volcanoes: Misti, Chachani, and Pichu Pichu. The city's historic center, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is renowned for its colonial-era buildings and beautiful plazas.
Rainbow Mountain
Rainbow Mountain, also known as Vinicunca or Montaña de Siete Colores, is a stunning natural wonder located in the Andes of Peru. This mountain is renowned for its vibrant, multicolored stripes, which are the result of mineral deposits over millions of years. The colors include red, yellow, green, and blue, creating a breathtaking and unique landscape.The mountain is situated at an altitude of approximately 5,200 meters (17,100 feet) above sea level
Kuélap
Kuélap, often referred to as "The Lost Citadel," is a remarkable archaeological site located in the mountains of northern Peru. Built by the Chachapoya culture around the 6th century AD, Kuélap is a walled settlement perched on a limestone ridge overlooking the Utcubamba Valley. The citadel covers an area of about six hectares and features massive limestone walls that reach heights of 10 to 20 meters
