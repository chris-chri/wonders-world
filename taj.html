Taj Mahal
The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most famous and beautiful monuments in the world. It was commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 as a mausoleum for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. The construction took about 22 years and was completed in 1653, involving thousands of artisans and craftsmen
Golden Temple
The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a revered Sikh gurdwara located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. Its construction began in 1581 under the direction of Guru Arjan, the fifth Sikh Guru, and was completed in 1604. The temple was built to serve as a central place of worship for Sikhs and to symbolize equality and openness, with four entrances welcoming people from all directions.
Gateway Of India
The Gateway of India is an iconic monument located in Mumbai, India. It was built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911. The foundation stone was laid in 1913, and the construction was completed in 1924. Designed by British architect George Wittet, the Gateway of India features an Indo-Saracenic architectural style, blending elements of Hindu, Muslim, and Gothic designs
Qutub Minar
Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India. Standing at a remarkable height of 73 meters (240 feet), it is the tallest brick minaret in the world and an iconic symbol of Indo-Islamic architecture. Construction began in 1199 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, and was completed by his successor, Iltutmish, in 1220
